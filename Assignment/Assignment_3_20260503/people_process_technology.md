# People, Process, and Technology in SOC

| Category     | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| People      | In a Security Operations Center (SOC), people include security analysts, incident responders, SOC managers, and threat hunters. They are trained to monitor, detect, and respond to security threats, ensuring human expertise complements automated systems. |
| Process     | Processes in SOC involve standardized procedures for threat detection, incident response, log analysis, and compliance. This includes workflows like triage, escalation, and post-incident reviews to maintain operational efficiency and effectiveness. |
| Technology  | Technology encompasses tools such as SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems, IDS/IPS (Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems), EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response), and automation platforms. These technologies enable real-time monitoring, threat intelligence integration, and rapid response to incidents. |



#### Technical Roles in a SOC

* __SOC Analysts (Tiers 1, 2, 3)__:
    * __Tier 1 (Triager)__: Monitors the queue, filters out noise, and identifies potential security incidents.
    * __Tier 2 (Responder)__: Conducts deeper investigations of incidents escalated by Tier 1, focusing on root cause analysis.
    * __Tier 3 (Hunter)__: Conducts deep investigations, forensics, and acts as a subject matter expert.
* __Security Engineers__: Build and configure the technical infrastructure (SIEM, EDR, firewalls) that the team relies on.
* __Threat Hunters__: Proactively seek hidden threats and malicious activity that automated systems miss.
* __Incident Responders__: Specialized personnel who lead the response to active, major security breaches, containing and remediating threats.
* __Forensic Analysts__: Perform deep forensic analysis of compromised systems.


#### Non-Technical & Strategic Roles in a SOC

These roles focus on management, process improvement, communication, and bridging technical findings with business risk.

* __SOC Manager__: Manages day-to-day operations, staffing, scheduling, training, and reporting to the CISO.

* __SOC Director / CISO__: Sets the overall strategy, aligns SOC objectives with business goals, and manages budget and compliance.

* __Compliance/Governance Officer__: Ensures that the SOC operates within regulatory frameworks (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, NIST).

* __Communications/Report Coordinator__: Prepares reporting and dashboards for non-technical stakeholders and leadership.
